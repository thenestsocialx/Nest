A reading

Missing being known

Three minutes

There is a kind of loneliness that has nothing to do with how many people are in your phone. It has to do with whether any of them know the shape of your week. Whether someone, somewhere, would notice if you went quiet for a Tuesday.

It often arrives in the company of others. You are at a dinner. The conversation is fine. You are laughing at the right places. And underneath, a small, exact ache: none of these people would know what to ask me, if they wanted to.

This is not a failure of yours. It is a particular feature of how we live now, in cities, in chats, in the soft tyranny of staying in touch without ever quite being in contact. It is also workable. Slowly. With a person.

If you've read this far and you've recognised yourself, that recognition is the first quiet thing. The next quiet thing is a person.
